## Findings that must control future claims

### 1. Quantum causal-order kinematics now exists; the physical law is not selected

The audit has crossed its earlier kinematic gate. Fixed-cardinality causal orders
are quotiented by genuine order isomorphism, complete unlabeled maximal-element
growth trajectories are constructed, and normalized amplitude laws generate an
exactly projective, strongly positive decoherence functional on cylinder events.
The chiral module also supplies a finite microscopic selector: equal Born weight
for the two first causal births forces the relative phase `+i` or `-i` and induces
the matching pure orientation kernel.

A physical quantum causal theory still needs at least:

- a derivation of elementary relation-complement symmetry from deeper dynamics;
- a microscopic origin for the nonzero reflection-odd source or boundary datum;
- a physical interpretation of the required uniform fallback, or an amplitude
  law that avoids the proved 14-event destructive zero;
- relational observables with an infrared interpretation on the history quotient;
- a nontrivial coarse-graining flow recovering ordinary GR and QFT.

The permitted claim is now **“projective quantum causal-set cylinder kinematics
with a balanced-birth endpoint theorem,”** not yet **“a complete quantum theory of
spacetime.”**

### 2. Actual link recovery remains a bridge condition

`CausalBridge.DirectionalDenseLinks` originally isolates the missing probabilistic
statement: actual links in increasingly dense sprinklings must occur near every null
direction. Its later unconditional witness chooses exact-null real vectors but does
not require them to be links drawn from a sprinkling.

Therefore:

- the algebraic implication “same null cone implies conformal relation” survives;
- the stochastic statement “the order supplies that null cone” remains open;
- a proof about arbitrary displacement vectors must not be cited as a sprinkling
  theorem.

### 5. T11 is a targeted negative, not a universal no-go theorem

The T11 experiment tested 500 reflection-symmetrized sprinklings with
`N ∈ {12, 14, 16, 18, 20}`. Its unbiased matching strategies did not approach `J₄`.
This rules out the tested direct claim that bare `K_F` on those sprinklings already
has the deterministic chamber spectrum.

It does **not** by itself rule out:

- a different causal-set operator;
- a renormalized or coarse-grained `K_F`;
- another ensemble or universality class;
- convergence visible only at substantially larger `N`.

Any follow-up must be preregistered so that operator, coarse-graining map, observable,
normalization, matching rule, sample sizes, and rejection threshold are fixed before
examining the result.

## Required 3+1D infrared program

### A. Microscopic dynamics

Choose one explicit dynamical route and keep the alternatives separate:

- a quantum sequential-growth/decoherence-functional model;
- a sum over unlabeled finite causal orders with an action;
- a transfer matrix or Hamiltonian on a causal-order state space.

quadratic observable.

### B. Coarse-graining map

Define an actual map

```text
microscopic causal state at scale N
    -> blocked/effective causal state at scale bN
    -> effective observables and couplings.
```

For `K_F`, this means computing a scale-dependent operator from each microscopic
sample and deriving the effective chamber projection after blocking or ensemble
averaging. The density/scale argument must occur in the definition.

### C. Universality tests

At minimum, compare multiple microscopic ensembles and initial conditions. A physical
infrared limit should depend on a universality class, not on the special deterministic
presentation `[m]^4` unless that presentation is itself dynamically selected.

## Claim policy

Use the following vocabulary consistently in source comments, papers, and README
material:

| Label | Permitted claim |
|---|---|
| **Finite theorem** | Exact result about the defined finite algebraic object |
| **Conditional bridge** | Consequence assuming a named geometric/probabilistic input |
| **Identification** | Physical interpretation supplied externally and tested against data |
| **Empirical support/negative** | Result of a reproducible numerical protocol |
| **Open target** | Required construction or theorem with no claimed witness |

“Zero `sorry`” certifies the stated proposition. It does not certify that the
proposition formalizes the intended physical claim. Every headline should therefore
name both its proof status and its physical scope.
