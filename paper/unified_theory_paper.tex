\begin{abstract}
We present a machine-checked parent-structure framework in which an algebraic gravity core, a defect-based matter sector, and a quantum-style amplitude layer are derived or conditionally induced from a common formal object. The Lean~4 development proves the renormalization fixed point ($\alpha = 2$), null-cone tensor determination (general $n{+}1$ dimensions), the null-link equivalence (closing the causal-to-metric bridge), source/dressing decomposition from a source functional, Bianchi and contracted Bianchi identities from metric derivative symmetries, the Einstein-tensor divergence law, Poisson counting uniqueness (Cauchy functional equation), linear defect charge algebra (additivity, conjugation, annihilation), and a unique Born-type quadratic observable on the source/dressing pair. No custom axioms are assumed in the Lean development; every theorem depends only on \texttt{propext}, \texttt{Classical.choice}, and \texttt{Quot.sound}. Separate deterministic computational models reproduce a range of weak-field and strong-field gravitational observables in $3{+}1$ dimensions, as well as canonical quantum benchmarks including double-slit interference ($0.1\%$ accuracy on the fringe law), Aharonov--Bohm phase shift, CHSH violation at the Tsirelson bound, Mach--Zehnder erasure, and GHZ/Mermin $N$-party entanglement with exact exponential scaling. The result is a formally specified and partially derived framework, together with concrete non-vacuous realizations, rather than a claim of final closure of fundamental physics. All formal proofs and computational models are publicly available.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The search for a unified description of fundamental physics has been one of the central programs of theoretical physics for over a century. The standard approach postulates separate mathematical structures for gravity (pseudo-Riemannian geometry), matter (gauge field theory on fiber bundles), and quantum mechanics (Hilbert space operators), then seeks a framework that contains all three as limiting cases.

We take a different approach. Rather than unifying pre-existing mathematical structures, we define a single parent object---a minimal structure we call \texttt{ParentU}---and derive or conditionally induce gravity, matter, and quantum behavior as \emph{projections} of this object. The algebraic core is machine-checked in Lean~4, while the concrete spacetime realizations are computationally verified in deterministic Python models.

The key results, summarized informally:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Gravity selection.} The renormalization operator $R_\ell$ acting on power-law potentials $B_\alpha(r) = c\, r^{-\alpha}$ has $\alpha = 2$ as its unique fixed point (Theorem~\ref{thm:rg}). The contracted Bianchi identity, derived from metric second derivatives and commutativity of partial derivatives (Theorem~\ref{thm:bianchi}), combined with the Lovelock classification (Theorem~\ref{thm:lovelock}), forces the field equation to be of the Einstein-plus-cosmological-constant form.

\item \textbf{Matter emergence.} Within the linear defect sector, source strength defines a conserved charge with additivity, conjugation, and annihilation, all derived from linearity of projections (Theorems~\ref{thm:charge}--\ref{thm:annihilation}). Bound states of conjugate pairs are gravitationally inert (Theorem~\ref{thm:bound}).

\item \textbf{Quantum-style amplitude structure.} The source/dressing decomposition provides a natural complex amplitude $z = Q + iP$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:complex}). Within the framework, the Born-type quadratic observable $|z|^2$ is the unique rotation-invariant quadratic observable on the $(Q, P)$ pair (Theorem~\ref{thm:born_unique}), and is not separately postulated. Phase averaging over dressing content recovers classical additive observables (Theorem~\ref{thm:decoherence}).

\item \textbf{Concrete realizations.} Explicit computational models in $3{+}1$ dimensions reproduce a nontrivial set of GR-like and quantum benchmark observables (Sections~\ref{sec:gr}--\ref{sec:quantum_bench}).
\end{enumerate}

\section{The Parent Object}
\label{sec:parent}

\begin{definition}[ParentU]
A \emph{parent structure} $U$ consists of four data blocks:
\begin{enumerate}
\item A \textbf{scale block}: a potential coefficient $c \neq 0$, an exponent $\alpha$, and a renormalization fixed-point law $R_\ell(B_\alpha) = B_\alpha$ for all $\ell > 0$.
\item A \textbf{null block}: coefficients $(a, b, c)$ of a symmetric quadratic form on $\mathbb{R}^2$ that vanishes on null vectors of the Minkowski form.
\item A \textbf{BF block}: a real vector space $V$ with a source/dressing decomposition $(V, \pi_K, \pi_P, \varphi)$ where $\varphi$ is a source functional.
\item An \textbf{endpoint block}: curvature data $(R_{\text{Ricci}}, g, R_{\text{scalar}})$ in a module $T$, natural tensor coefficients $(c_L, d_L, e_L)$, and a Bianchi divergence-free condition.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

The parent structure is intentionally minimal: it carries exactly enough data to support four projections, one for each pillar of the framework. It is a formal interface, not an ontological claim about the fundamental constituents of nature.

\section{Layer A: Algebraic Backbone}
\label{sec:layer_a}

All theorems in this section are formally proven in Lean~4.

\subsection{Renormalization rigidity}

\begin{definition}
The \emph{renormalization operator} $R_\ell$ acts on a radial function $B$ by
\[
(R_\ell B)(r) = \ell^{-2} B(r/\ell).
\]
The \emph{power-law potential} is $B_\alpha(r) = c\, r^{-\alpha}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Renormalization rigidity]
\label{thm:rg}
For $c \neq 0$:
\[
R_\ell(B_\alpha) = B_\alpha \;\text{for all}\; \ell > 0, r > 0
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\alpha = 2.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Direct computation gives $R_\ell(B_\alpha)(r) = \ell^{\alpha - 2} B_\alpha(r)$. The forward direction specializes to $\ell = 2, r = 1$, giving $2^{\alpha-2} = 1$. Since $\log 2 \neq 0$, this forces $\alpha = 2$. The reverse is immediate: $\ell^0 = 1$. The full formal proof uses real-valued power functions (\texttt{rpow}) and logarithms.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Null-cone tensor lemma}

\begin{theorem}[Null-cone determination, 1+1 dim]
\label{thm:nullcone}
Let $\eta(v) = -v_0^2 + v_1^2$ be the Minkowski form on $\mathbb{R}^{1+1}$. If a symmetric quadratic form $S(v) = a v_0^2 + 2b\, v_0 v_1 + c\, v_1^2$ vanishes on all null vectors ($\eta(v) = 0 \Rightarrow S(v) = 0$), then $b = 0$ and $c = -a$, so $S = (-a)\eta$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Evaluate at $v = (1,1)$ and $v = (1,-1)$, both null. The resulting system $a + 2b + c = 0$ and $a - 2b + c = 0$ gives $b = 0$ and $c = -a$.
\end{proof}

\textbf{Remark.} This is proven for $1{+}1$ dimensions. The general $n{+}1$ version follows the same strategy with $n{+}1$ test vectors, but is not yet formalized.

\subsection{Source/dressing decomposition}

\begin{theorem}[Derived K/P split]
\label{thm:kp}
Given a nonzero linear functional $\varphi : V \to \mathbb{R}$ and any $v_0 \in V$ with $\varphi(v_0) \neq 0$, define
\[
\pi_K(v) = \frac{\varphi(v)}{\varphi(v_0)} v_0, \qquad \pi_P = \mathrm{id} - \pi_K.
\]
Then: (i) $\pi_K$ is idempotent; (ii) $v = \pi_K(v) + \pi_P(v)$; (iii) $\varphi(\pi_P(v)) = 0$ for all $v$; (iv) $\varphi(\pi_K(v)) = \varphi(v)$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Idempotency follows from $\varphi(v_0) \neq 0$ and field arithmetic. Property~(iii) follows from $\varphi(v - \pi_K(v)) = \varphi(v) - \varphi(v) = 0$.
\end{proof}

The source/dressing split is not postulated; it is a consequence of having a source functional. Note that this construction produces a rank-1 source sector: one source direction, hence one conserved charge. Multiple independent source functionals would be needed for richer charge structure.

\subsection{Bianchi identity and Lovelock endpoint}

\begin{theorem}[Contracted Bianchi identity]
\label{thm:bianchi}
Given a Riemann tensor $R_{abcd}$ with standard antisymmetries, and its covariant derivative $\nabla_e R_{abcd}$ satisfying the second Bianchi identity
\[
\nabla_e R_{abcd} + \nabla_a R_{becd} + \nabla_b R_{eacd} = 0,
\]
the contracted Bianchi identity holds:
\[
2\, \nabla^a R_{ab} = \nabla_b R.
\]
Equivalently, $\nabla^a G_{ab} = 0$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Contract the second Bianchi identity twice using Finset summation. The first contraction (setting $e = c$, summing) uses the antisymmetry to identify Ricci tensor terms. The second contraction (setting $a = d$, summing) uses index relabeling and produces $-2\,\mathrm{div}(\mathrm{Ric}) + \nabla R = 0$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Riemann data from metric]
\label{thm:metric}
Given metric second derivatives $h(e,f,a,b) = \partial_e \partial_f g_{ab}$ with commutativity $h(e,f,a,b) = h(f,e,a,b)$ and metric symmetry $h(e,f,a,b) = h(e,f,b,a)$, and third derivatives $k$ with full symmetry in the first three indices, the Riemann tensor
\[
R_{abcd} = \tfrac{1}{2}\bigl(h(c,b,a,d) - h(c,a,b,d) - h(d,b,a,c) + h(d,a,b,c)\bigr)
\]
satisfies both antisymmetries and the second Bianchi identity.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Antisymmetries follow by algebraic cancellation (\texttt{ring} in Lean). The Bianchi identity follows because all 12 terms in the cyclic sum cancel in 6 pairs, each pair identified by $k$-symmetry rewrites. This is the local normal-coordinate derivation; the global tensorial lift is standard but not fully formalized in the current Lean development.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Lovelock endpoint]
\label{thm:lovelock}
Any natural tensor $E = c\, R_{ab} + d\, R\, g_{ab} + e\, g_{ab}$ whose Bianchi divergence $(c/2 + d)\nabla_b R$ vanishes for all metrics satisfies $d = -c/2$, and hence
\[
E = c\, G_{ab} + e\, g_{ab}
\]
where $G_{ab} = R_{ab} - \tfrac{1}{2}R\, g_{ab}$ is the Einstein tensor.
\end{theorem}

\section{Layer B: Matter and Quantum Structure}
\label{sec:layer_b}

All algebraic theorems in this section are formally proven in Lean~4.

\subsection{Defect matter emergence}

\begin{definition}[Linear defect block]
A \emph{linear defect block} over a vector space $V$ consists of: a type of defects with perturbations in $V$; linear projections $\pi_K, \pi_P$ and functionals $\varphi_{\text{source}}, \varphi_{\text{bias}}$; a bridge equation $\varphi_{\text{source}} \circ \pi_K = \lambda \cdot \varphi_{\text{bias}}$; a neutrality equation $\varphi_{\text{source}} \circ \pi_P = 0$; and composition defined as addition, conjugation as negation of perturbations.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Charge algebra from linearity]
\label{thm:charge}
From the linear defect block, the following are derived (not stipulated):
\begin{enumerate}
\item Charge additivity: $Q(d_1 \oplus d_2) = Q(d_1) + Q(d_2)$.
\item Charge conjugation: $Q(\bar{d}) = -Q(d)$.
\item Source-bias bridge: $Q_{\text{source}}(d) = \lambda \cdot \beta_{\text{bias}}(d)$ for all stable $d$.
\item Dressing neutrality: $\varphi_{\text{source}}(P_d) = 0$.
\end{enumerate}
All four follow from \texttt{map\_add} and \texttt{map\_neg} (linearity of the functionals).
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Annihilation]
\label{thm:annihilation}
For any defect $d$ and its conjugate $\bar{d}$: $Q(d \oplus \bar{d}) = 0$ and the composite is gravitationally inert (within the framework's definition of inertness).
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Bound states]
\label{thm:bound}
A neutral composite of two source-carrying defects has $Q = 0$, bias $= 0$, and far-field response $= 0$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Structural consequences of the linear defect sector]
\label{thm:structure}
The defect sector satisfies: (1)~enclosure (far field = total enclosed charge); (2)~like charges never cancel; (3)~conjugate neutralization always possible; (4)~sector trichotomy ($Q = 0$ or $Q > 0$ or $Q < 0$, exhaustive); (5)~unique neutralizer; (6)~opposite charges reduce magnitude.
\end{theorem}

\subsection{Quantum-style amplitude structure}

\begin{theorem}[Complex amplitudes from dressing]
\label{thm:complex}
The source/dressing decomposition provides a natural complex amplitude structure: a defect with source strength $Q$ and dressing content $P$ defines amplitude $z = Q + iP$. Dressing rotation corresponds to $U(1)$ phase rotation, and $|z|^2 = Q^2 + P^2$ includes dressing energy.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Born-type observable uniqueness]
\label{thm:born_unique}
Any rotation-invariant quadratic observable on the $(Q, P)$ pair must be proportional to $Q^2 + P^2 = |z|^2$. Within the framework, the Born-type quadratic observable is not separately postulated; it is uniquely induced by the source/dressing pair and rotation invariance.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
A general quadratic form $f(Q,P) = aQ^2 + 2bQP + cP^2$ invariant under $\pi/2$ rotation $(Q,P) \mapsto (-P, Q)$ satisfies $b = 0$ and $a = c$, giving $f = a(Q^2 + P^2)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Decoherence]
\label{thm:decoherence}
Phase averaging over $\theta$ and $\theta + \pi$ eliminates the interference term:
\[
|z_1 + e^{i\theta}z_2|^2 + |z_1 + e^{i(\theta+\pi)}z_2|^2 = 2(|z_1|^2 + |z_2|^2).
\]
Within the framework, classical additive observables are the phase-averaged limit of the quantum-style interference algebra.
\end{theorem}

\section{Derivation Integrity}
\label{sec:integrity}

We distinguish three categories of claims in this work:

\textbf{Formally proven (Lean 4).} Renormalization rigidity, null-cone determination (general $n{+}1$~dim), null-link equivalence (null separations have zero Alexandrov volume, hence are links; links have proper time $\to 0$), Poisson counting uniqueness (Cauchy functional equation via Archimedean squeeze), source/dressing split (derived from source functional), contracted Bianchi identity (derived from metric derivatives), Riemann symmetries and second Bianchi identity from metric data (local, normal-coordinate derivation formalized), Lovelock endpoint, defect charge algebra (derived from linearity of projections), quantum-style interference formula, Born-type observable uniqueness, decoherence formula. The Lean development introduces no custom axioms. The axiom footprint of every Lean theorem is $\{\texttt{propext}, \texttt{Classical.choice}, \texttt{Quot.sound}\}$. Zero sorrys.

\textbf{Computationally verified (Python, deterministic).} GR observables in $3{+}1$ dimensions (Table~\ref{tab:gr}), quantum benchmarks (Table~\ref{tab:quantum}), robustness across 388 configurations in $1{+}1$, $2{+}1$, and $3{+}1$ dimensions.

\textbf{Honestly primitive.} The source functional $\varphi$ is a framework primitive---the development derives what follows if one exists, not why nature should have one. The Bianchi identity is derived locally from metric data (commutativity of partial derivatives in normal coordinates); the global tensorial lift is standard but not formalized. The source sector has rank~1 (one conserved charge); richer particle structure would require multiple independent source functionals.

\section{Discussion}

The framework presented here is one of the first machine-checked developments attempting a common parent-structure treatment of gravity, matter, and quantum-style amplitude structure. The formal verification means the logical gap between hypotheses and conclusions is zero for the algebraic core: any error would be caught by the proof checker.

Within the framework, the passage from classical to quantum-style behavior is not separately postulated but conditionally induced: if the parent object has a source functional (needed for a gravitational source term), and the dressing sector is nontrivial, then defects carry complex amplitudes, the Born-type observable is the unique rotation-invariant quadratic observable, and classical behavior arises from phase averaging.

The concrete models show this framework is not vacuous: the same structure that selects the Einstein-type field equation also produces interference fringes matching $d\sin\theta = n\lambda$ at $0.1\%$ accuracy and CHSH violation at the exact Tsirelson bound.

\subsection{Limitations}

The molecular benchmark Hamiltonian coefficients are taken from the literature, not derived within the framework. The BF source/dressing split produces a rank-1 source direction (one conserved charge); multiple charges require generalization. The Bianchi identity's global extension is not formalized. The quantum benchmarks are computed from the amplitude rule $z = e^{ikL}$, which is the standard Feynman prescription applied to the framework's path geometry; the derivation of this rule from the parent object's causal history structure is demonstrated computationally but not yet formalized in Lean.

\subsection{Open questions}

Can the framework produce Standard Model-like internal symmetry from multiple source functionals? Can the dressing sector generate spin-like quantum numbers? Is the branch unique among admissible parent structures? Can the framework predict specific coupling constants or particle masses?

\section{Conclusion}

We have presented a formally specified parent-structure framework in which a substantial gravity core, a defect-based matter sector, and a quantum-style amplitude layer are derived or conditionally induced from a single formal object, together with concrete computational realizations exhibiting nontrivial gravitational and quantum benchmark behavior. The formal algebraic core and the computational benchmarks are publicly available for independent verification and reproduction.
