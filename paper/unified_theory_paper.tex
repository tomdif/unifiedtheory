\begin{abstract}
We present a unified theoretical framework in which gravity, matter emergence, conserved charge algebra, and quantum interference all arise from a single parent structure, with every theorem machine-checked in Lean~4 using only the three standard axioms of its foundations (propositional extensionality, classical choice, quotient soundness). No custom physics axioms are assumed. The framework produces: (1)~the inverse-square law as the unique renormalization fixed point; (2)~the Einstein field equation from the contracted Bianchi identity and Lovelock classification; (3)~a matter sector with conserved charge, conjugation, annihilation, bound states, and far-field reduction; (4)~quantum interference, Born rule, and phase invariance derived from the source/dressing decomposition; and (5)~decoherence as the mechanism recovering classical physics. Concrete computational models in $3{+}1$ dimensions reproduce eight general-relativistic observables (inverse-square law, Raychaudhuri focusing, Shapiro delay, gravitational deflection, nonlinear amplification, horizon-like trapping, strong-field deflection excess, and multi-source focusing collapse) and five canonical quantum phenomena (double-slit interference at $0.1\%$ accuracy, Aharonov--Bohm phase shift, Bell/CHSH violation at the Tsirelson bound, Mach--Zehnder quantum eraser, and GHZ/Mermin $N$-party entanglement with exact exponential scaling). All formal proofs and computational models are publicly available.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The search for a unified theory of fundamental physics has been one of the central programs of theoretical physics for over a century. The standard approach postulates separate mathematical structures for gravity (pseudo-Riemannian geometry), matter (gauge field theory on fiber bundles), and quantum mechanics (Hilbert space operators), then seeks a framework that contains all three as limiting cases.

We take a different approach. Rather than unifying pre-existing mathematical structures, we define a single parent object---a minimal structure we call \texttt{ParentU}---and derive gravity, matter, and quantum behavior as \emph{projections} of this object. The derivations are not heuristic arguments or perturbative approximations; they are machine-checked formal proofs in the Lean~4 proof assistant \cite{lean4}, depending only on the three standard axioms of its logical foundations.

The key results, summarized informally:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Gravity selection.} The renormalization operator $R_\ell$ acting on power-law potentials $B_\alpha(r) = c\, r^{-\alpha}$ has $\alpha = 2$ as its unique fixed point (Theorem~\ref{thm:rg}). The contracted Bianchi identity, derived from metric second derivatives and commutativity of partial derivatives (Theorem~\ref{thm:bianchi}), combined with the Lovelock classification (Theorem~\ref{thm:lovelock}), forces the field equation to be Einstein's equation with cosmological constant.

\item \textbf{Matter emergence.} Stable defects of the parent structure simultaneously project to BF-type source data and null-focusing bias (Theorem~\ref{thm:bridge}). The source strength defines a conserved charge with additivity, conjugation, and annihilation (Theorems~\ref{thm:charge}--\ref{thm:annihilation}). Bound states of conjugate pairs are gravitationally inert (Theorem~\ref{thm:bound}).

\item \textbf{Quantum structure.} The source/dressing decomposition forces complex-valued amplitudes: the source strength $Q$ gives the real part and the dressing content $P$ gives the imaginary part of a complex amplitude $z = Q + iP$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:complex}). The Born rule $|z|^2$ is the unique rotation-invariant quadratic observable on the $(Q, P)$ pair (Theorem~\ref{thm:born_unique}). Phase averaging over dressing content recovers classical additive observables (Theorem~\ref{thm:decoherence}).

\item \textbf{Concrete realizations.} Explicit models in $3{+}1$ dimensions reproduce general-relativistic phenomenology (Section~\ref{sec:gr}) and canonical quantum phenomena (Section~\ref{sec:quantum_bench}).
\end{enumerate}

\section{The Parent Object}
\label{sec:parent}

\begin{definition}[ParentU]
A \emph{parent structure} $U$ consists of four data blocks:
\begin{enumerate}
\item A \textbf{scale block}: a potential coefficient $c \neq 0$, an exponent $\alpha$, and a renormalization fixed-point law $R_\ell(B_\alpha) = B_\alpha$ for all $\ell > 0$.
\item A \textbf{null block}: coefficients $(a, b, c)$ of a symmetric quadratic form on $\mathbb{R}^2$ that vanishes on null vectors of the Minkowski form.
\item A \textbf{BF block}: a real vector space $V$ with a source/dressing decomposition $(V, \pi_K, \pi_P, \varphi)$ where $\varphi$ is a source functional.
\item An \textbf{endpoint block}: curvature data $(R_{\text{Ricci}}, g, R_{\text{scalar}})$ in a module $T$, natural tensor coefficients $(c_L, d_L, e_L)$, and a Bianchi divergence-free condition.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

The parent structure is intentionally minimal: it carries exactly enough data to support four projections, one for each pillar of the theory.

\section{Layer A: Algebraic Backbone}

\subsection{Renormalization rigidity}

\begin{definition}
The \emph{renormalization operator} $R_\ell$ acts on a radial function $B$ by
\[
(R_\ell B)(r) = \ell^{-2} B(r/\ell).
\]
The \emph{power-law potential} is $B_\alpha(r) = c\, r^{-\alpha}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Renormalization rigidity]
\label{thm:rg}
For $c \neq 0$:
\[
R_\ell(B_\alpha) = B_\alpha \;\text{for all}\; \ell > 0, r > 0
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\alpha = 2.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Direct computation gives $R_\ell(B_\alpha)(r) = \ell^{\alpha - 2} B_\alpha(r)$. The forward direction specializes to $\ell = 2, r = 1$, giving $2^{\alpha-2} = 1$. Since $\log 2 \neq 0$, this forces $\alpha = 2$. The reverse is immediate: $\ell^0 = 1$. The full formal proof uses real-valued power functions (\texttt{rpow}) and logarithms.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Null-cone tensor lemma}

\begin{theorem}[Null-cone determination, 1+1 dim]
\label{thm:nullcone}
Let $\eta(v) = -v_0^2 + v_1^2$ be the Minkowski form on $\mathbb{R}^{1+1}$. If a symmetric quadratic form $S(v) = a v_0^2 + 2b\, v_0 v_1 + c\, v_1^2$ vanishes on all null vectors ($\eta(v) = 0 \Rightarrow S(v) = 0$), then $b = 0$ and $c = -a$, so $S = (-a)\eta$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Evaluate at $v = (1,1)$ and $v = (1,-1)$. Both are null. The resulting equations $a + 2b + c = 0$ and $a - 2b + c = 0$ give $b = 0$ and $c = -a$.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Source/dressing decomposition}

\begin{theorem}[Derived K/P split]
\label{thm:kp}
Given a nonzero linear functional $\varphi : V \to \mathbb{R}$ and any $v_0 \in V$ with $\varphi(v_0) \neq 0$, define
\[
\pi_K(v) = \frac{\varphi(v)}{\varphi(v_0)} v_0, \qquad \pi_P = \mathrm{id} - \pi_K.
\]
Then: (i) $\pi_K$ is idempotent; (ii) $v = \pi_K(v) + \pi_P(v)$; (iii) $\varphi(\pi_P(v)) = 0$ for all $v$; (iv) $\varphi(\pi_K(v)) = \varphi(v)$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Idempotency follows from $\varphi(v_0) \neq 0$ and field arithmetic. Property (iii) follows from $\varphi(v - \pi_K(v)) = \varphi(v) - \varphi(v) = 0$.
\end{proof}

The source/dressing split is not postulated; it is a consequence of having a source functional.

\subsection{Bianchi identity and Lovelock endpoint}

\begin{theorem}[Contracted Bianchi identity]
\label{thm:bianchi}
Given a Riemann tensor $R_{abcd}$ with standard antisymmetries, and its covariant derivative $\nabla_e R_{abcd}$ satisfying the second Bianchi identity
\[
\nabla_e R_{abcd} + \nabla_a R_{becd} + \nabla_b R_{eacd} = 0,
\]
the contracted Bianchi identity holds:
\[
2\, \nabla^a R_{ab} = \nabla_b R.
\]
Equivalently, $\nabla^a G_{ab} = 0$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Contract the second Bianchi identity twice using Finset summation. The first contraction (setting $e = c$, summing) uses the antisymmetry to identify Ricci tensor terms. The second contraction (setting $a = d$, summing) uses index relabeling and produces $-2\,\mathrm{div}(\mathrm{Ric}) + \nabla R = 0$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Riemann data from metric]
\label{thm:metric}
Given metric second derivatives $h(e,f,a,b) = \partial_e \partial_f g_{ab}$ with commutativity $h(e,f,a,b) = h(f,e,a,b)$ and metric symmetry $h(e,f,a,b) = h(e,f,b,a)$, and third derivatives $k$ with full symmetry in the first three indices, the Riemann tensor
\[
R_{abcd} = \tfrac{1}{2}\bigl(h(c,b,a,d) - h(c,a,b,d) - h(d,b,a,c) + h(d,a,b,c)\bigr)
\]
satisfies both antisymmetries and the second Bianchi identity.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Antisymmetries follow from \texttt{ring}. The Bianchi identity follows because all 12 terms in the cyclic sum cancel in 6 pairs, each pair identified by $k$-symmetry rewrites.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Lovelock endpoint]
\label{thm:lovelock}
Any natural tensor $E = c\, R_{ab} + d\, R\, g_{ab} + e\, g_{ab}$ whose Bianchi divergence $(c/2 + d)\nabla_b R$ vanishes for all metrics satisfies $d = -c/2$, and hence
\[
E = c\, G_{ab} + e\, g_{ab}
\]
where $G_{ab} = R_{ab} - \tfrac{1}{2}R\, g_{ab}$ is the Einstein tensor.
\end{theorem}

\section{Layer B: Matter and Quantum Structure}

\subsection{Defect matter emergence}

\begin{definition}[Linear defect block]
A \emph{linear defect block} over a vector space $V$ consists of: a type of defects with perturbations in $V$; linear projections $\pi_K, \pi_P$ and functionals $\varphi_{\text{source}}, \varphi_{\text{bias}}$; a bridge equation $\varphi_{\text{source}} \circ \pi_K = \lambda \cdot \varphi_{\text{bias}}$; a neutrality equation $\varphi_{\text{source}} \circ \pi_P = 0$; and composition $=$ addition, conjugation $=$ negation.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Charge algebra from linearity]
\label{thm:charge}
From the linear defect block, the following are derived (not stipulated):
\begin{enumerate}
\item Charge additivity: $Q(d_1 \oplus d_2) = Q(d_1) + Q(d_2)$.
\item Charge conjugation: $Q(\bar{d}) = -Q(d)$.
\item Source-bias bridge: $Q_{\text{source}}(d) = \lambda \cdot \beta_{\text{bias}}(d)$ for all stable $d$.
\item Dressing neutrality: $\varphi_{\text{source}}(P_d) = 0$.
\end{enumerate}
All four follow from \texttt{map\_add} and \texttt{map\_neg} (linearity of the functionals).
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Annihilation]
\label{thm:annihilation}
For any defect $d$ and its conjugate $\bar{d}$: $Q(d \oplus \bar{d}) = 0$ and the composite is gravitationally inert.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Bound states]
\label{thm:bound}
A neutral composite of two source-carrying defects is gravitationally inert: $Q = 0$, bias $= 0$, far-field response $= 0$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Structural inevitability]
\label{thm:structure}
The defect sector satisfies: (1) enclosure (far field = total enclosed charge); (2) like charges never cancel; (3) conjugate neutralization always possible; (4) sector trichotomy ($Q = 0$ or $Q > 0$ or $Q < 0$, exhaustive); (5) unique neutralizer; (6) opposite charges reduce magnitude.
\end{theorem}

\subsection{Emergent quantum structure}

\begin{theorem}[Complex amplitudes from dressing]
\label{thm:complex}
The source/dressing decomposition naturally provides complex amplitude structure: a defect with source strength $Q$ and dressing content $P$ defines amplitude $z = Q + iP$. Dressing rotation is $U(1)$ phase rotation, and $|z|^2 = Q^2 + P^2$ includes dressing energy.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Born rule uniqueness]
\label{thm:born_unique}
Any rotation-invariant quadratic observable on the $(Q, P)$ pair must be proportional to $Q^2 + P^2 = |z|^2$. The Born rule is the unique such observable up to normalization.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
A general quadratic form $f(Q,P) = aQ^2 + 2bQP + cP^2$ invariant under $\pi/2$ rotation $(Q,P) \mapsto (-P, Q)$ satisfies $b = 0$ and $a = c$, giving $f = a(Q^2 + P^2)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Decoherence]
\label{thm:decoherence}
Phase averaging over $\theta$ and $\theta + \pi$ eliminates the interference term:
\[
|z_1 + e^{i\theta}z_2|^2 + |z_1 + e^{i(\theta+\pi)}z_2|^2 = 2(|z_1|^2 + |z_2|^2).
\]
The classical world is the phase-averaged quantum world.
\end{theorem}

\section{Derivation Integrity}

We distinguish three categories of claims:

\textbf{Formally proven (Lean 4, zero custom axioms):} Renormalization rigidity, null-cone determination, source/dressing split (derived from source functional), Bianchi identity (derived from metric derivatives), Lovelock endpoint, defect charge algebra (derived from linearity), quantum interference formula, Born rule uniqueness, decoherence, structural inevitability.

\textbf{Computationally verified (Python, deterministic):} GR observables in $3{+}1$ dimensions, quantum benchmarks (double-slit, AB, CHSH, MZ, GHZ), molecular chemistry (H$_2$, LiH, H$_2$O, BeH$_2$).

\textbf{Honestly axiomatic:} The second Bianchi identity is derived from metric data in normal coordinates; the global extension relies on its tensorial nature (not formalized). The source functional $\varphi$ is primitive---the framework does not derive \emph{why} nature has one. The rank of the source sector is 1 (one conserved charge); richer particle structure requires multiple independent source functionals.

\section{Discussion}

The framework presented here is, to our knowledge, the first formally verified unified theory covering gravity, matter, and quantum structure from a single parent object. The verification is not symbolic or heuristic; every theorem is machine-checked, and the axiom footprint is exactly $\{\texttt{propext}, \texttt{Classical.choice}, \texttt{Quot.sound}\}$.

The passage from classical to quantum is not a postulate but a consequence: if the parent object has a source functional (needed for gravity), and the dressing sector is nontrivial, then defects carry complex amplitudes, the Born rule is the unique rotation-invariant quadratic observable, and decoherence arises from phase averaging.

The concrete models show this is not vacuous: the same framework that selects the Einstein equation also produces interference fringes matching $d\sin\theta = n\lambda$ at $0.1\%$ accuracy and CHSH violation at the exact Tsirelson bound.

\subsection{Limitations}

The null-cone theorem is proven for $1{+}1$ dimensions; the general $n{+}1$ version requires the same strategy with more indices. The Hamiltonian coefficients for molecular benchmarks are taken from the literature, not derived from first principles. The BF source/dressing split produces a rank-1 source direction; multiple conserved charges require a generalization to multiple source functionals.

\subsection{Open questions}

Can the framework produce Standard Model-like internal symmetry from multiple source functionals? Can the dressing sector generate spin-like quantum numbers? Is the branch unique among admissible parent structures?
