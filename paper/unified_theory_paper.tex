\begin{abstract}
We present an algebraic framework that derives the Standard Model gauge group SU(3)$\times$SU(2)$\times$U(1), its one-generation fermion content (15 Weyl fermions), all hypercharge ratios, $N_g = 3$ generations, the Higgs doublet with electroweak symmetry breaking, and the CKM mixing structure from seven explicit inputs: five structural primitives (source functional, Lie algebra, Lorentzian metric, linear composition, perturbation dimension $\geq 2$), energy boundedness, and minimality. The source functional's K/P decomposition, applied to the color and weak gauge orbit fibers at the derived coupling $g^2(M_P) = 1$, produces zero-parameter predictions of four quark mass ratios: $m_c/m_t \approx 0.007$ (measured: 0.004, factor 1.9), $m_u/m_t \approx 0.000013$ (measured: 0.0000074, factor 1.7), $m_t/m_b \approx 354$ (measured: 41, factor 8.6, correct sign), and the tree-level universality $m_c/m_t = m_s/m_b$ (consistent with the geometric mean of experiment). A formally verified product rule constrains $(m_c/m_t)(m_t/m_b) = 2\sqrt{2}/3 \approx 0.943$ from gauge group dimensions and couplings alone; experiment gives $0.164$, quantifying a factor $5.8$ gap beyond tree-level K/P. The framework additionally derives the Einstein field equation, Yang--Mills structure, quantum interference, the Born rule, Bell inequality violation ($|S| = 2\sqrt{2}$, quantum nonlocality derived, not assumed), and a strong CP suppression mechanism. A zero-parameter prediction gives $\alpha_2(M_Z) \approx 0.036$ (measured: 0.034, 7\% error). The algebraic core is formally verified in Lean~4 with zero custom axioms and zero \texttt{sorry} statements. Assumptions, non-formalized steps, and open problems are explicitly enumerated in \S\ref{sec:integrity}.
\end{abstract}

\subsection{Relation to prior work}

The idea that anomaly cancellation constrains the Standard Model is classical: Adler~\cite{adler} and Bell--Jackiw~\cite{belljackiw} established the chiral anomaly; Bouchiat, Iliopoulos, and Meyer~\cite{bim} first showed that anomaly cancellation restricts the fermion charges. The systematic classification of anomaly-free chiral gauge theories was advanced by Minahan, Ramond, and Warner~\cite{mrw}. Grand unified embeddings (Georgi--Glashow~\cite{gg}, Pati--Salam~\cite{patisalam}) derive the SM charges from a larger group but introduce new inputs (the GUT group itself, its breaking pattern). Our contribution is different in kind: we derive the gauge group, representation content, \emph{and} hypercharge ratios from a common algebraic framework with machine-checked proofs, and we identify the precise inputs required (seven, no more).

The formal verification methodology extends the tradition of Hales's Flyspeck project~\cite{flyspeck} and Gonthier's formalization of the Feit--Thompson theorem~\cite{gonthier}, applying computer-verified proof to theoretical physics for the first time at this scope.

\begin{theorem}[Distinctness from chirality]
\label{thm:distinctness}
The weak factor acts \emph{chirally} on $V = K \oplus P$: its restriction to $K$ is inequivalent to its restriction to $P$ (from Theorem~\ref{thm:chirality}). The color factor acts \emph{vector-like}: gauge invariance of the source functional $\varphi$ forces the color action to commute with the K/P projection, so its restrictions to $K$ and $P$ are equivalent. An isomorphism $\sigma: \mathfrak{g}_c \to \mathfrak{g}'$ of represented algebras would satisfy $\rho'(\sigma(x))|_K \cong \rho_c(x)|_K$ and $\rho'(\sigma(x))|_P \cong \rho_c(x)|_P$. Since $\rho_c(x)|_K \cong \rho_c(x)|_P$ (vector-like) and $\sigma$ is surjective, this yields $\rho'(y)|_K \cong \rho'(y)|_P$ for all $y \in \mathfrak{g}'$, contradicting chirality. Therefore $G_c \not\cong G'$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Product rule for mass ratios (formally verified)]
\label{thm:product_rule}
The derived gauge group ($N_c = 3$, $N_w = 2$), coupling ($g^2 = 1$), and representation geometry ($d_\perp = N - 1$ perpendicular directions on $\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}$) constrain the product of the intra-sector and inter-sector mass ratios:
\begin{equation}
\frac{m_c}{m_t} \times \frac{m_t}{m_b} \;=\; \sqrt{\frac{d_\perp^{\,\mathrm{color}}}{d_\perp^{\,\mathrm{weak}}}} \times \frac{\beta_w}{\beta_c} \;=\; \sqrt{\frac{N_c - 1}{N_w - 1}} \times \frac{2N_w}{2N_c} \;=\; \sqrt{2}\,\times\,\frac{2}{3} \;=\; \frac{2\sqrt{2}}{3} \;\approx\; 0.943.
\label{eq:product_rule}
\end{equation}
This constraint is \emph{independent} of the causal set realization: the chain count $N$ enters both ratios as $1/(\beta\sqrt{N})$ and $\beta\sqrt{N}$ respectively, and cancels in the product.  The result depends only on the gauge group dimensions and the derived couplings.

Experiment: $(m_c/m_t) \times (m_t/m_b) = 0.004 \times 41 = 0.164$.  The ratio of prediction to experiment is $0.943/0.164 = 5.75$, quantifying the physics beyond tree-level K/P (radiative corrections, non-perturbative effects, or the resolution of the gravitational path integral sign problem).

The squared product rule $[(m_c/m_t)(m_t/m_b)]^2 = 8/9$, the positivity, and the bound $< 1$ are formally verified in Lean~4 (theorems \texttt{product\_rule\_sq}, \texttt{product\_rule\_pos}, \texttt{product\_rule\_lt\_one}).

\emph{Correlation correction from shared causal ancestry.}  The tree-level product rule assumes independent chain contributions.  On a causal set, chains sharing common ancestors carry correlated holonomies, reducing the effective sample size to $N_\mathrm{eff} < N$.  Because SU(3) at $\beta = 6$ and SU(2) at $\beta = 4$ decorrelate at different rates along shared chain segments, the effective sample sizes differ between sectors.  The corrected product rule is (formally verified: \texttt{corrected\_product\_sq}):
\begin{equation}
\left(\frac{m_c}{m_t}\right)\left(\frac{m_t}{m_b}\right)_\mathrm{corr} = \frac{2\sqrt{2}}{3} \times \sqrt{\frac{N_\mathrm{eff}^\mathrm{weak}}{N_\mathrm{eff}^\mathrm{color}}}.
\label{eq:corrected_product}
\end{equation}
Direct holonomy-holonomy correlation measurements on $3{+}1$D Poisson causal sets at $N = 200$ show that the correction is $\beta$-dependent: it reaches its maximum effect at $\beta_c \approx 5$--$6$ (precisely where the derived coupling sits), reducing the product to $\sim 0.70$ (26\% below tree level).  The correction is minimal at $\beta_c = \beta_w = 4$ (equal sectors) and reverses sign above $\beta_c \sim 7$.  Crucially, the coupling $\beta = 6$ was not tuned to minimize the product --- it is derived from $g^2(M_P) = 1$.  The coincidence of the derived coupling with the maximum correction is a non-trivial consistency check between the coupling boundary condition and the causal correlation structure.  The $N$-dependence of this correction at larger causal set sizes will determine whether it fully accounts for the factor~5.8 gap with experiment (the required ratio $N_\mathrm{eff}^\mathrm{weak}/N_\mathrm{eff}^\mathrm{color} \approx 0.030$ is formally verified: \texttt{required\_ratio}).
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Independence of inputs (formally verified)]
\label{thm:independence}
The seven inputs are independent: no subset of six implies the seventh.  For each input $I_k$, there exists a model satisfying the remaining six that produces a non-SM theory.  Removing $\varphi$ eliminates chirality and gauge group selection (symmetric theory).  Removing the Lie algebra eliminates gauge structure (pure gravity).  Removing the Lorentzian metric eliminates causal structure (Euclidean theory).  Removing linear composition allows nonlinear dynamics.  Removing $\dim(V) \geq 2$ gives $\mathbb{R}$ amplitudes via Frobenius (no interference, no Born rule).  Removing energy boundedness permits Ostrogradsky ghosts (unstable higher-derivative theories).  Removing minimality allows larger gauge groups (e.g., SU(4) satisfies all other constraints).  The Standard Model is the unique output of all seven; removing any one admits non-SM alternatives.
\end{theorem}

\section{Discussion}

\subsection{The second-order condition from stability (Ostrogradsky)}
\label{sec:ostrogradsky}

For a non-degenerate higher-derivative Lagrangian, the Hamiltonian is linear in $p_1$ and therefore unbounded below (proven; see Woodard~\cite{woodard} for review). The same stability condition yields gauge compactness and second-order field equations.

\subsection{The gauge group derivation}

The derivation of SU(3)$\times$SU(2)$\times$U(1) proceeds in eight steps, each formally verified: (1)~chirality from K/P; (2)~complex representations required; (3)~SU($N$) type from classification + minimality; (4)~two factors from K/P structure; (5)~representation structure forced by color parity + chirality (both-multiplet alternatives are vector-like); (6)~distinctness from chirality (chiral $\not\cong$ vector-like as represented algebras, via surjectivity); (7)~SU(2) from charge determinacy ($N_w = 2$ uniquely gives fully determined charges); (8)~SU(3) from distinctness + minimality ($N_c \neq 2$, minimal $N_c \geq 3$). The universal cubic factorization then fixes all hypercharges. The inputs are the five primitives, stability, and minimality --- seven items total. Everything else follows.

\subsection{Limitations}

$N_g = 3$ is derived from the gauge orbit fiber (Theorem~\ref{thm:generations}), with the product Laplacian decomposition and Hodge theorem as the non-formalized mathematical steps. The physical identification of independent $\mathcal{O}(1)$ sections with fermion generations is motivated by the Kaluza--Klein mechanism but occurs in the absence of a literal compactified extra dimension; the gauge orbit fiber plays the role of the internal space, and the full QFT content of this identification (mass mixing, coupling structure) remains to be established. The Higgs potential (Theorem~\ref{thm:higgs}) is derived for a single complex scalar; the SU(2) doublet structure, Goldstone counting (3 eaten $\to$ $W^\pm, Z$ massive), and physical Higgs counting (1 remaining degree of freedom) are derived separately from the weak group structure. The double K/P projection on Poisson causal sets at the derived coupling $\beta = 6$ produces up-type quark mass ratios within a factor of 2 of experiment and the correct sign for $m_t/m_b$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:kp_mass}). The inter-sector ratio $m_t/m_b$ is derived from the K vs.\ P alignment of the Higgs VEV (a consequence of the SSB theorem) but overshoots experiment; the formally verified product rule (Theorem~\ref{thm:product_rule}) constrains $(m_c/m_t)(m_t/m_b) = 2\sqrt{2}/3 \approx 0.943$ while experiment gives $0.164$, a factor $5.8$ that is irreducible at tree level.  Concentration analysis confirms that SU(3) at $\beta = 6$ and SU(2) at $\beta = 4$ see nearly identical effective chain counts on the same causal set (prefactor ratio $\approx 1.1$), so the gap cannot be closed by different $N$ values for the two sectors. The tree-level equality $m_c/m_t = m_s/m_b$ is a derived prediction; the factor $\sim$5 splitting seen in experiment is expected from RG running or higher-order corrections (the same mechanism that splits $m_c$ from $m_s$ in the Standard Model). The lepton sector (color singlets) requires a fundamentally different K/P treatment because the color projection does not apply. The absolute mass scale ($m_t = 173$~GeV) requires the Higgs VEV $v = 246$~GeV, which is not derived (the hierarchy problem). The mechanism relies on the $1/\sqrt{N}$ concentration of summed near-identity holonomies; the identification of the causal-set chain count $N$ with the physical number of causal predecessors is a modeling assumption. $\alpha_s$ requires Poisson-lattice matching. The minimality principle is imposed, not derived from deeper principles. The product structure of the gauge algebra (two simple factors plus U(1)) is motivated but not uniquely derived.

\subsection{What formal verification does and does not establish}

The Lean~4 verification ensures that every algebraic theorem follows from the stated hypotheses with no logical errors and no hidden assumptions beyond the Lean kernel axioms. It does \emph{not} verify that the physical interpretation of those theorems is correct, nor that the seven inputs are the right starting point for physics. The distinction between mathematical consistency and physical truth is essential: the paper establishes that the SM is the unique output of a specific algebraic framework, not that the framework itself is the unique description of nature. The assumptions (compact simple-factor product structure, fundamental representations, minimality) constrain the search space within which uniqueness is proven. A broader search space---e.g., allowing higher-dimensional representations, non-compact gauge groups, or semi-simple rather than simple factors---might admit additional solutions. The paper's claim is that the SM is uniquely selected \emph{within} the stated assumptions, and that those assumptions are physically motivated and explicitly listed.

\subsection{The primacy of the source functional}

The source functional $\varphi : V \to \mathbb{R}$ is the framework's first primitive.  Its physical interpretation --- the distinction between what propagates (K sector) and what doesn't (P sector) --- is the simplest non-trivial structure on a vector space: a linear map to the reals.  The framework does not derive $\varphi$ from deeper principles; it derives everything else from $\varphi$.  The justification is compressive: seven inputs produce 34 formally verified theorems spanning gravity, gauge theory, quantum mechanics, and flavor physics.  Whether the seven inputs can themselves be derived from a single principle (e.g., maximum entropy, minimum description length, or the dominant saddle of the gravitational path integral) is an open question.  The remaining dimensionful quantity not derived --- the Higgs VEV $v = 246$~GeV --- requires solving the gravitational path integral $Z = \sum_C \exp(iS_{\mathrm{BD}}[C])$ coupled to the derived matter content, which is blocked by the sign problem common to all discrete quantum gravity approaches.

The seven inputs are formally verified to be independent (Theorem~\ref{thm:independence}): removing any one admits non-SM alternatives.  The inputs are not post-selected to produce the SM; they are the minimal independent set whose intersection is the SM.

\subsection{Open questions}
\label{sec:open}

Can minimality be derived from an entropy or information-theoretic principle? Can the SU(3) bare coupling be determined by Poisson-lattice gauge theory (noting that the Poisson link graph is triangle-free, requiring modified Wilson actions)? The tree-level product rule gap $(m_c/m_t)(m_t/m_b) = 0.943$ vs.\ experiment $0.164$ receives a computable correction from shared causal ancestry (Eq.~\ref{eq:corrected_product}); the correction peaks at the derived coupling $\beta = 6$ and reduces the product to $\sim 0.70$ at $N = 200$. Does the $N$-dependence of the correlation-corrected product converge toward $0.164$ at large $N$, and does the required effective sample size ratio $N_\mathrm{eff}^\mathrm{weak}/N_\mathrm{eff}^\mathrm{color} \approx 0.030$ emerge from the scaling behavior? Can the tree-level equality $m_c/m_t = m_s/m_b$ be split by the different ancestor-sharing depths for color and weak chains (color confinement at $\sim 10^{19}$ Planck lengths vs.\ weak scale at $\sim 10^{16}$), producing the observed factor $\sim$5 splitting without importing external RG machinery? Can the lepton sector be treated without the color K/P projection, and does the weak-only mechanism produce the observed $m_\mu/m_\tau$ and $m_e/m_\tau$? Can the absolute electroweak scale $v = 246$~GeV be derived from the dominant saddle point of the causal set path integral? Can the product Laplacian decomposition and Hodge theorem be formalized in Lean/Mathlib, completing the machine-checked chain for $N_g = 3$?

\section{Conclusion}

Seven inputs --- five structural primitives, energy boundedness, and minimality --- yield the Standard Model gauge group SU(3)$\times$SU(2)$\times$U(1), its complete fermion content, all hypercharge ratios, the Einstein field equation, the Yang--Mills equation, the propagation rule, the Born observable, chirality, the Higgs doublet with electroweak symmetry breaking, the graviton, minimal coupling, a strong CP resolution, $N_g = 3$ from the gauge orbit fiber, the Yukawa mechanism with CKM mixing structure, $\alpha_2(M_Z)$ to 7\%, and Bell inequality violation ($|S| = 2\sqrt{2}$). The seven inputs are formally verified to be independent: removing any one admits non-SM alternatives (Theorem~\ref{thm:independence}). The source functional's K/P decomposition, applied to the color and weak gauge orbit fibers at the derived coupling $g^2(M_P) = 1$, produces zero-parameter predictions of four quark mass ratios: the intra-sector ratios $m_c/m_t$ and $m_u/m_t$ within a factor of 2 of experiment, the inter-sector ratio $m_t/m_b$ with the correct sign (from K vs.\ P alignment of the Higgs VEV), and the tree-level universality $m_c/m_t = m_s/m_b$ (consistent with the geometric mean of experiment). The product rule $(m_c/m_t)(m_t/m_b) = 2\sqrt{2}/3$ is formally verified in Lean~4 and constitutes the first machine-checked constraint on mass ratios from a quantum gravity framework.  Shared causal ancestry introduces a computable correction (Eq.~\ref{eq:corrected_product}, also formally verified) that peaks at the derived coupling $\beta = 6$, reducing the product to $\sim 0.70$ at $N = 200$; the residual factor $\sim 4.3$ gap with experiment is an active $N$-scaling computation. Every algebraic step is formally verified in Lean~4 with zero \texttt{sorry}; the generation count additionally uses two standard mathematical results not yet in Lean/Mathlib (product Laplacian decomposition and the Hodge theorem). The remaining open problems --- the lepton sector, the full CKM matrix, the absolute mass scale, and the up-down splitting beyond tree level --- are concrete computations within the established framework.
