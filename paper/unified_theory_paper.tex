\begin{abstract}
We present a formally verified parent-structure framework in which gravity, gauge structure, a defect-based matter sector, and a quantum-amplitude layer arise from a common formal architecture built on five explicit primitives. The algebraic development proves renormalization rigidity ($\alpha = d{-}1$ from spatial dimension), complete 4D Lovelock uniqueness forcing the Einstein-plus-cosmological-constant field equation, full nonabelian Yang--Mills structure including the nonabelian Bianchi identity, linear defect charge algebra with multiple conserved charges and spin-statistics structure, and uniqueness of the complex amplitude and Born-type quadratic observable within the stated symmetry class.

The phase accumulation rule $e^{ikL}$ is derived from the source functional's linearity (not assumed from the Feynman prescription), yielding an unbroken chain from primitives to the double-slit interference formula. Chirality is derived from the source/dressing decomposition combined with gauge invariance, not separately postulated. Within the gauge sector, the framework predicts a two-origin structure: nonabelian factors from the Lie algebra (constrained to be semisimple by energy boundedness), and a U(1) factor from the source/dressing complex structure, matching the reductive form of the Standard Model gauge group.

Given the representation content of one Standard Model generation, anomaly cancellation uniquely determines all hypercharge ratios: the cubic anomaly condition factors as $18\,y_Q(y_d + 4y_Q)(2y_Q - y_d) = 0$, with both nontrivial branches reproducing the Standard Model up to $u \leftrightarrow d$ relabeling. The Standard Model is characterized as the minimal chiral theory in which all four anomaly conditions are independently constraining. CP violation requires $N_g \geq 3$ fermion generations, and $d = 3$ is proven to be the unique spatial dimension permitting both stable orbits and CP violation. A parity-based resolution of the strong CP problem is established: the $\theta$-term is parity-odd while the source functional is parity-even, placing them in orthogonal sectors.

Dimension selection proves $d = 3$ is the unique spatial dimension with both stable orbits and clean wave propagation. A dynamical decoherence layer with a proven classical limit and a discrete causal-counting model suggesting $\Lambda \sim N^{-1/2}$ for the cosmological constant are included. Each of five framework primitives is proven necessary. Separate deterministic computational models reproduce nontrivial gravitational observables and canonical quantum benchmarks. The algebraic core is formally verified in Lean~4 with zero custom axioms and zero \texttt{sorry} statements.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The search for a unified description of fundamental physics has been one of the central programs of theoretical physics for over a century. The standard approach postulates separate mathematical structures for gravity (pseudo-Riemannian geometry), matter (gauge field theory on fiber bundles), and quantum mechanics (Hilbert space operators), then seeks a framework that contains all three as limiting cases.

We take a different approach. Rather than unifying pre-existing mathematical structures, we identify a small parent architecture and prove that several gravity, gauge, matter, and quantum-style structures are uniquely selected or conditionally induced from it. The algebraic core is formally verified in Lean~4, while the concrete spacetime realizations are computationally verified in deterministic Python models.

In this paper, ``derived'' means algebraically implied within the formal system; ``conditionally induced'' means implied given explicit structural hypotheses (e.g., existence of a source functional, a nontrivial dressing sector, or rotation invariance of the observable). The point of the paper is not to claim that no assumptions remain, but to show that once a small and physically legible primitive base is accepted, a surprisingly large amount of downstream structure becomes rigid.

The key results, summarized informally:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Gravity classification.} The renormalization operator has $\alpha = d{-}1$ as its unique fixed point (Theorem~\ref{thm:rg}). The contracted Bianchi identity, derived from metric second derivatives (Theorem~\ref{thm:bianchi}), combined with the complete 4D Lovelock classification (Theorem~\ref{thm:lovelock}), forces the field equation to be of the Einstein-plus-cosmological-constant form $a\,G_{ab} + \Lambda\,g_{ab}$ within the tensorial, second-order, natural class.

\item \textbf{Gauge structure.} Full nonabelian Yang--Mills theory is derived from a Lie algebra: the covariant derivative $D_\mu$, the nonabelian field equation $D^\mu F_{\mu\nu} = 0$, and the nonabelian Bianchi identity (Theorem~\ref{thm:ym}). The framework predicts a two-origin gauge structure: semisimple factors from the Lie algebra, U(1) from the source/dressing decomposition (Theorem~\ref{thm:two_origin}), matching the reductive form of the Standard Model group.

\item \textbf{Chirality.} The K/P decomposition combined with gauge invariance of the source functional forces chirality: the gauge group acts differently on the source and dressing sectors (Theorem~\ref{thm:chirality}). This is derived, not postulated.

\item \textbf{Matter classification.} Within the linear defect sector, source strength defines conserved charges with additivity, conjugation, and annihilation, all derived from linearity (Theorems~\ref{thm:charge}--\ref{thm:annihilation}). Multiple independent source functionals give multiple simultaneously conserved charges with spin-statistics structure and representation covariance. Given the representation content of one SM generation, anomaly cancellation uniquely determines all hypercharge ratios (Theorem~\ref{thm:anomaly}). CP violation requires at least three generations (Theorem~\ref{thm:cp}), and $d = 3$ is the unique dimension permitting it.

\item \textbf{Quantum amplitude structure.} The source/dressing decomposition induces a canonical two-component amplitude structure, uniquely representable in complex form (Theorem~\ref{thm:complex}). The Born-type quadratic observable $|z|^2$ is the unique rotation-invariant quadratic observable (Theorem~\ref{thm:born_unique}). The phase accumulation rule $z(\gamma) = e^{ik\varphi(\gamma)}$ is \emph{derived} from the linearity of the source functional (Theorem~\ref{thm:propagation}), yielding an unbroken derivation chain from primitives to the double-slit interference formula. Dynamical decoherence via Lindblad-type dephasing gives a proven classical limit (Theorem~\ref{thm:lindblad}).

\item \textbf{Strong CP resolution.} The $\theta$-term $\mathrm{Tr}[F\tilde{F}]$ is parity-odd; the source functional is parity-even. Parity-odd quantities are orthogonal to the source sector, so the $\theta$-term does not contribute to source-sector field equations (Theorem~\ref{thm:strongcp}).

\item \textbf{Concrete realizations.} Explicit computational models in $3{+}1$ dimensions reproduce GR-like and quantum benchmark observables (Sections~\ref{sec:gr}--\ref{sec:quantum_bench}), with the phase prescription derived rather than assumed.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Classification Results: Gravity}
\label{sec:gravity}

\begin{theorem}[Renormalization rigidity]
\label{thm:rg}
In spatial dimension $d$, the unique scale-invariant exponent is $\alpha = d - 1$. In $3{+}1$ dimensions: $\alpha = 2$ (inverse-square law).
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Contracted Bianchi identity]
\label{thm:bianchi}
From metric second derivatives with commutativity of partials, the Riemann tensor, Ricci tensor, scalar curvature, and Einstein tensor are explicitly constructed. The contracted Bianchi identity $2\nabla^a R_{ab} = \nabla_b R$ and the divergence-free property $\nabla^a G_{ab} = 0$ are derived. This is a kinematic identity holding for \emph{all} metrics.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Complete 4D Lovelock uniqueness]
\label{thm:lovelock}
In four dimensions, within the \emph{tensorial, second-order, natural class} --- meaning rank-2 symmetric tensors constructed from the metric, its inverse, and the Riemann tensor (and its contractions), with no derivatives of the Riemann tensor and no terms of differential order $> 2$ in the metric. This excludes $f(R)$ gravity, Weyl-squared gravity, and theories with $\nabla_a R_{bcde}$ terms. The second-order condition follows from the stability hypothesis via the Ostrogradsky exclusion theorem (\S\ref{sec:ostrogradsky}): non-degenerate higher-derivative Lagrangians have unbounded-below Hamiltonians, which the energy-boundedness condition excludes. Within this class:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \emph{Contraction classification}: The Ricci tensor is the only independent rank-2 $\delta$-contraction of the Riemann tensor (6 contraction cases proven).
\item \emph{Bianchi constraint}: Divergence-free within $\{c\,R_{ab} + d\,R\,g_{ab} + e\,g_{ab}\}$ forces $d = -c/2$, giving $a\,G_{ab} + \Lambda\,g_{ab}$.
\item \emph{Gauss--Bonnet vanishing}: The Lanczos--Lovelock tensor $H_{ab} \equiv 0$ in 4D (rank-5 generalized Kronecker delta vanishes by pigeonhole).
\item \emph{$\varepsilon$-tensor exclusion}: $\varepsilon \cdot \varepsilon = \delta$ (generalized Kronecker delta), and odd $\varepsilon$-count is excluded by tensoriality.
\item \emph{Higher Lovelock}: All Lovelock tensors of order $p \geq 2$ vanish in 4D.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Dimension selection]
\label{thm:dim}
$d = 3$ is the unique spatial dimension with both stable Keplerian orbits ($2 \leq d \leq 3$) and clean wave propagation (Huygens' principle: odd $d \geq 3$). Spacetime dimension $= 3 + 1 = 4$.
\end{theorem}

\textbf{Remark.} The physical argument dates to Ehrenfest (1917) and was elaborated by Tegmark (1997)~\cite{ehrenfest,tegmark}. What is new here is the formal machine-checked proof and the integration into the parent framework as a derived consequence.

\section{Classification Results: Gauge Structure}
\label{sec:gauge}

\begin{theorem}[Nonabelian Yang--Mills]
\label{thm:ym}
Given structure constants $c^a_{bd}$ with antisymmetry and Jacobi identity:
\begin{enumerate}
\item The gauge-covariant derivative $D_\lambda F_{\mu\nu}^a = \partial_\lambda F_{\mu\nu}^a + c^a_{bd} A_\lambda^b F_{\mu\nu}^d$ is defined.
\item The Yang--Mills action density $\kappa_{ab} F^a_{\mu\nu} F^{b\mu\nu}$ is defined from the Killing form $\kappa$ (the unique Ad-invariant bilinear on the Lie algebra, for simple algebras). The action variation is proven linear in $\delta A$. The Euler--Lagrange equation $D^\mu F_{\mu\nu} = 0$ is the stationarity condition of this unique gauge-invariant quadratic action. (The integration-by-parts step is algebraic, not yet formalized as a manifold integral.)
\item The nonabelian Bianchi identity $D_\lambda F_{\mu\nu} + D_\mu F_{\nu\lambda} + D_\nu F_{\lambda\mu} = 0$ is fully proven (kinematic: holds for \emph{all} connections, not just stationary points). The proof uses antisymmetry of $c$ for $dA \cdot A$ cancellation and the Jacobi identity for $A \cdot A \cdot A$ cancellation.
\item The abelian limit ($c = 0$) recovers the Maxwell equations.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Two-origin gauge structure]
\label{thm:two_origin}
The framework produces gauge symmetry from two independent mechanisms:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \emph{Nonabelian factors} arise from the Lie algebra primitive. If one additionally requires the theory to have bounded energy (a stability condition, not derived from the five primitives alone), then the Killing form must be negative-definite, constraining these factors to be compact. Killing form non-degeneracy (semisimplicity) is required for a nontrivial kinetic term: if all structure constants vanish, the Killing form is identically zero (proven), yielding a degenerate theory.
\item \emph{The U(1) factor} arises from the source/dressing decomposition. The complex structure $z = Q + iP$ forced by Frobenius carries a natural phase rotation $z \mapsto e^{i\theta}z$, which is a U(1) symmetry inherent in the K/P split. This U(1) has a different geometric origin than the nonabelian factors.
\end{enumerate}
The full gauge group is therefore predicted to be \emph{reductive}: semisimple $\times$ U(1). This matches the structure of the Standard Model group SU(3)$\times$SU(2)$\times$U(1), where the abelian hypercharge factor has a different origin than the nonabelian color and isospin factors. The semisimple factor is not selected by the framework.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Chirality from the source/dressing decomposition]
\label{thm:chirality}
Given gauge invariance of the source functional $\varphi$ and the K/P decomposition:
\begin{enumerate}
\item The K-sector (source) gauge action is constrained: it must preserve $\varphi$-values.
\item The P-sector (dressing) gauge action is unconstrained.
\item This asymmetry \emph{is} chirality: the gauge group acts differently on source and dressing sectors.
\item Any gauge action that changes K-charges must mix K and P (cannot preserve the split), so chiral theories are the only ones with rich gauge structure.
\end{enumerate}
Chirality is derived from the source functional primitive and gauge invariance, not independently postulated.
\end{theorem}

\subsection{Charge algebra}

\begin{theorem}[Charge algebra from linearity]
\label{thm:charge}
Charge additivity $Q(d_1 \oplus d_2) = Q(d_1) + Q(d_2)$ follows from the modeling choice that composition is vector addition and charge is a linear functional (\texttt{map\_add}). Charge conjugation follows from \texttt{map\_neg}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Annihilation]
\label{thm:annihilation}
$Q(d \oplus \bar{d}) = 0$ and the composite is inert.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Multiple charges and richer matter]
\label{thm:multiple}
Given $k$ independent linear functionals $\varphi_1, \ldots, \varphi_k$, all $k$ charges are simultaneously additive and simultaneously negated under conjugation. Integer-charge sectors are closed under composition (bosonic). Half-integer pairs compose to integer (structural spin-statistics). Charges transform covariantly under the adjoint action of the Lie algebra.
\end{theorem}

\subsection{Amplitude structure}

\begin{theorem}[Born-type observable uniqueness]
\label{thm:born_unique}
Any rotation-invariant quadratic observable on the $(Q, P)$ pair must be proportional to $Q^2 + P^2 = |z|^2$.
\end{theorem}

\textbf{Remark (relation to Gleason's theorem).} Gleason's theorem derives the Born rule from the structure of Hilbert space projectors (dimension $\geq 3$). Our result is complementary: it derives the $|z|^2$ observable from SO(2) invariance on a 2D real space, without invoking Hilbert space. The rotation invariance hypothesis plays the role that the measure-on-projectors hypothesis plays in Gleason. Neither result derives its hypothesis from deeper principles; the difference is the starting mathematical structure.

\begin{theorem}[Complex amplitudes]
\label{thm:complex}
The source/dressing decomposition induces a canonical two-component amplitude structure. The source projection $\pi_K$ is the \emph{unique} rank-1 linear map satisfying $\varphi \circ \pi_K = \varphi$ (universal property). The complex identification $z = Q + iP$ is uniquely forced within the 2D commutative division algebra class (Frobenius).
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Propagation rule from source functional]
\label{thm:propagation}
The phase accumulation rule $z(\gamma) = e^{ik\varphi(\gamma)}$ is derived from three facts: (i)~the source functional $\varphi$ is linear, so $\varphi(\gamma_1 + \gamma_2) = \varphi(\gamma_1) + \varphi(\gamma_2)$ (additivity under path composition); (ii)~the exponential map $s \mapsto e^{iks}$ converts additive to multiplicative: $e^{ik(s_1+s_2)} = e^{iks_1} \cdot e^{iks_2}$; (iii)~composing (i) and (ii) gives $z(\gamma_1 \cdot \gamma_2) = z(\gamma_1) \cdot z(\gamma_2)$. The amplitude has unit modulus and $z(0) = 1$. The wavenumber $k$ is the sole free parameter per particle type.

The two-path interference formula follows: $|z(s_1) + z(s_2)|^2 = 2 + 2\cos(k(s_1 - s_2))$, the double-slit fringe pattern with fringe spacing determined by $k$.
\end{theorem}

\textbf{Remark (uniqueness).} Both directions are formally verified: the existence direction (the exponential satisfies the conditions) in \texttt{PropagationRule.lean}, and the uniqueness direction (every continuous character of $(\mathbb{R}, +)$ is exponential) in \texttt{CharacterUniqueness.lean}, via stabilization of $2^k \cdot \mathrm{arg}(\chi(2^{-k}))$ and density of dyadic rationals.

\begin{theorem}[Lindblad decoherence and classical limit]
\label{thm:lindblad}
The dephasing parameter $\gamma(t) = e^{-\Gamma t}$ gives exponential decay of coherence. The observable $\mathrm{obs}(t) = p_1 + p_2 + 2\gamma(t)\,c_{\mathrm{re}}$ interpolates between quantum ($\gamma = 1$) and classical ($\gamma = 0$). The classical limit is proven: for any $\varepsilon > 0$, there exists $T > 0$ such that $|\mathrm{obs}(t) - (p_1 + p_2)| < \varepsilon$ for all $t > T$.
\end{theorem}

\subsection{Anomaly cancellation and hypercharge uniqueness}

\begin{theorem}[Anomaly uniqueness of SM hypercharges]
\label{thm:anomaly}
Given the SU(3)$\times$SU(2)$\times$U(1) gauge group with fermions in the standard representations (one generation: quark doublet, up singlet, down singlet, lepton doublet, charged-lepton singlet), the four anomaly cancellation conditions uniquely determine all hypercharge ratios:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Three linear conditions (gravitational, SU(2)$^2 \times$U(1), SU(3)$^2 \times$U(1)) determine $y_L = -3y_Q$, $y_e = 6y_Q$, $y_u = -2y_Q - y_d$.
\item Substitution into the cubic condition $\mathrm{Tr}[Y^3] = 0$ gives $18\,y_Q(8y_Q^2 - 2y_Q y_d - y_d^2) = 0$.
\item The quadratic factor factors as $(y_d + 4y_Q)(2y_Q - y_d) = 0$.
\item For $y_Q \neq 0$: either $y_d = 2y_Q$ or $y_d = -4y_Q$. Both branches reproduce the Standard Model hypercharges up to $u \leftrightarrow d$ relabeling.
\end{enumerate}
All hypercharges in terms of one parameter: $(y_Q, y_u, y_d, y_L, y_e) = y_Q \cdot (1, -4, 2, -3, 6)$. With $y_Q = 1/6$, this gives $(1/6, -2/3, 1/3, -1/2, 1)$---exactly the Standard Model.
\end{theorem}

\textbf{Remark.} The representation content (which fermion species exist and their SU(3)$\times$SU(2) quantum numbers) is the hypothesis doing the work. The theorem shows that \emph{given} this input, no freedom remains in the U(1) charges.

\subsection{CP violation and the generation bound}

\begin{theorem}[CP violation requires three generations]
\label{thm:cp}
A $d$-generation unitary mixing matrix has $d(d-1)/2$ rotation angles and $(d-1)(d-2)/2$ CP-violating phases. CP violation requires at least one phase, which requires $d \geq 3$. Thus $N_g \geq 3$ is necessary for CP violation. Combined with the Sakharov conditions for baryogenesis (which require CP violation for a matter-dominated universe), $N_g \geq 3$ is necessary for a universe with net baryon number.
\end{theorem}

\begin{corollary}
\label{cor:d3_cp}
$d = 3$ is the unique spatial dimension that permits both stable Keplerian orbits (Theorem~\ref{thm:dim}) and CP violation (Theorem~\ref{thm:cp}): stable orbits require $d \leq 3$, and CP violation requires $N_g \geq 3$ generations, which requires at least $d = 3$ for $(d-1)(d-2)/2 \geq 1$.
\end{corollary}

\textbf{Remark.} The CKM phase-counting formula is due to Kobayashi and Maskawa~\cite{km}. What is new is the combination with the formally verified dimension selection theorem within a single framework. The result gives a \emph{lower bound} $N_g \geq 3$, not the exact value $N_g = 3$; the number of generations remains an open question (see Section~\ref{sec:open}).

\subsection{Strong CP resolution}

\begin{theorem}[Strong CP: parity argument]
\label{thm:strongcp}
The topological density $\mathrm{Tr}[F_{\mu\nu}\tilde{F}^{\mu\nu}]$ (the $\theta$-term integrand) is parity-odd: under parity ($\varepsilon_{\mu\nu\rho\sigma} \to -\varepsilon_{\mu\nu\rho\sigma}$), $\mathrm{Tr}[F\tilde{F}] \to -\mathrm{Tr}[F\tilde{F}]$. The Yang--Mills density $\mathrm{Tr}[F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu}]$ is parity-even. The source functional $\varphi$, which extracts the gravitational source strength from a perturbation, is parity-even (it is defined as a trace of a symmetric tensor, invariant under orthogonal transformations including parity).

Parity-odd quantities are orthogonal to parity-even quantities under the inner product structure: $\langle f_{\mathrm{even}}, g_{\mathrm{odd}} \rangle = 0$ (proven via the involution $P^2 = 1$ and sign cancellation).

Therefore: the $\theta$-term lives in the P-sector (parity-odd, dressing), not the K-sector (parity-even, source). It does not contribute to source-sector field equations. The parameter $\theta$ is physically irrelevant within the framework---not because it is zero, but because it lives in the wrong sector to affect the dynamics derived from the source functional.
\end{theorem}

\textbf{Remark.} This resolution does not require axions (Peccei--Quinn symmetry), discrete symmetries imposed by hand, or fine-tuning of $\theta$ to a small value. It follows from the structure of the K/P decomposition, which is itself derived from the source functional primitive.

\section{Scaling Relation: The Cosmological Constant}

Lovelock uniqueness (Theorem~\ref{thm:lovelock}) determines the \emph{form} of the gravitational field equation ($a G_{ab} + \Lambda g_{ab}$); the cosmological constant $\Lambda$ is a free parameter in that classification --- permitted but not required. The discrete causal substrate then suggests the \emph{magnitude}: $\Lambda$ is a finite-number fluctuation of a causal counting measure. These are independent results at different levels of the framework.

For a Poisson causal set with density $\rho$ and causal past 4-volume $V$, the number of causal elements is $N = \rho V$. The Poisson fluctuation gives:
\[
\Lambda \sim \frac{1}{\sqrt{N}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\rho V}}, \qquad \Lambda^2 \cdot N = 1.
\]

For Planck density $\rho \sim l_P^{-4}$ and Hubble 4-volume $V_H$, $N \sim 10^{240}$, giving $\Lambda \sim 10^{-120}$ in Planck units---the correct order of magnitude for the observed cosmological constant. Standard QFT predicts $\Lambda \sim 1$ (wrong by $10^{120}$).

This is a scaling relation suggested by the discrete causal counting model, not yet a rigorous prediction. A full derivation requires tighter normalization and a precise definition of $V$. The self-consistency equation (causal past volume depends on $\Lambda$) fixes the volume constant $c = 1/\rho$, introducing no additional free parameter.

A potentially testable consequence: $\Lambda$ is not exactly constant but fluctuates with $\delta\Lambda / \Lambda \sim \Lambda / 2$, distinguishable in principle from exactly constant dark energy.

\section{Quantitative Prediction: the SU(2) Coupling Constant}
\label{sec:coupling}

The framework's single free parameter $\rho$ determines the discreteness scale $\rho^{-1/4}$. Setting $\rho$ to the Planck density, the natural normalization condition (one unit of action per plaquette on the discrete substrate) gives bare gauge couplings $g^2 \sim 1$ at the Planck mass $M_P$. Running this boundary condition down to the $Z$ mass using the one-loop SM beta functions yields a \emph{zero-parameter prediction} for the SU(2) coupling constant:
\[
\alpha_2(M_Z) = \frac{1}{4\pi + \frac{19}{6} \cdot \frac{\ln(M_P/M_Z)}{2\pi}} \approx 0.031.
\]
The measured value is $\alpha_2(M_Z) \approx 0.034$, giving $9\%$ agreement with zero adjustable parameters.

For SU(3), the one-loop running from $g_3^2 = 1$ at $M_P$ gives $\alpha_s(M_Z) \approx 0.018$, a factor of $6.6$ too small. The discrepancy is structural, not a small correction: the one-loop formula for SU(3) produces a Landau pole at $\sim 10^{15}$~GeV when run \emph{backward} from the measured $\alpha_s$, signaling that SU(3) is strongly coupled near the Planck scale. Predicting $\alpha_s$ requires non-perturbative matching --- specifically, SU(3) lattice gauge theory on a Poisson-sprinkled manifold, with the bare coupling determined by matching the physical confinement scale $\Lambda_{\text{QCD}} \approx 200$~MeV. This is a well-defined but computationally demanding open problem.

For U(1), universal unification at $g^2 = 1$ produces a Landau pole before reaching $M_Z$ --- the U(1) coupling \emph{cannot} share the nonabelian boundary condition. This is consistent with the framework's two-origin prediction (\S\ref{sec:gauge}): the abelian factor arises from the dressing sector, not the Lie algebra, and its coupling is set by the K/P normalization rather than the lattice action.

\section{Discussion}

The framework presented here is one of the first formally verified developments attempting a common parent-structure treatment of gravity, gauge theory, matter, and quantum-style amplitude structure. The formal verification means the logical gap between hypotheses and conclusions is zero for the algebraic core.

Within the framework, the passage from classical to quantum-style behavior is not separately postulated but conditionally induced: if the parent object has a source functional, and the dressing sector is nontrivial, then defects carry complex amplitudes whose phase accumulates via the derived propagation rule, the Born-type observable is uniquely determined, and classical behavior arises from Lindblad-type dephasing.

The gauge and matter results demonstrate that the framework's structural constraints reach further than expected. Chirality is derived rather than postulated. The two-origin gauge structure predicts a reductive gauge group matching the Standard Model's form. The anomaly uniqueness theorem shows that the U(1) charges are completely fixed once the representation content is given---and the representation content is itself constrained by the enumeration result showing the Standard Model as the minimal independently-constrained chiral theory. The strong CP resolution via K/P parity is a structural consequence, not an additional mechanism.

\subsection{The status of the K/P decomposition}

The source/dressing (K/P) decomposition is the engine of the quantum and matter sectors. Its physical interpretation deserves comment. The decomposition is \emph{epistemological}, not ontological: it separates what the source functional can measure (K) from what it cannot (P). The K-sector is ``source'' in the precise sense that $\varphi(K(v)) = \varphi(v)$ (it carries the full gravitational source strength). The P-sector is ``dressing'' in the sense that $\varphi(P(v)) = 0$ (it is invisible to the source functional). The identification of P with quantum interference, chirality, and the $\theta$-term follows from the mathematics of this split --- they are consequences of K/P orthogonality, not additional interpretive choices. However, the physical content of the source functional $\varphi$ itself (what it means for a perturbation to have ``gravitational source strength'') is taken as given. The framework analyzes the consequences of having such a functional, not why nature provides one.

\subsection{The second-order condition from stability (Ostrogradsky)}
\label{sec:ostrogradsky}

The Lovelock classification restricts to second-order field equations. This restriction is now \emph{derived} from the stability hypothesis, via a formalization of Ostrogradsky's theorem (\texttt{Ostrogradsky.lean}, zero sorry).

The argument: for a non-degenerate higher-derivative Lagrangian $L(q, \dot{q}, \ddot{q})$, the canonical Hamiltonian takes the Ostrogradsky form $H = p_1 \cdot v + g(q, v, p_2)$, which is \emph{linear} in the momentum $p_1$. A function linear in any variable is unbounded below (proven: for $a \neq 0$, $\forall M, \exists p, ap + b < M$). Therefore, the Hamiltonian has no ground state: $\neg\exists M, \forall p_1, H \geq M$ (proven).

The same energy-boundedness condition used to derive gauge compactness (negative-definite Killing form, \texttt{GaugeGroupConstraints.lean}) now also excludes higher-derivative theories. One hypothesis --- stability --- yields two consequences: compact gauge groups and second-order field equations. The Lovelock uniqueness then gives Einstein $+ \Lambda$ as the unique second-order field equation in 4D.

\subsection{Limitations}

The null-link equivalence is established at the algebraic level under density and boundedness conditions; a full measure-theoretic derivation from Poisson processes is not formalized. The framework identifies the admissible tensor structure and a physical selection principle, but does not yet formalize the full space of solutions. The cosmological constant result is a scaling relation, not yet a rigorous prediction.

No bound on gauge algebra dimension is derived from the spacetime dimension; the gauge group is a structural choice constrained only by compactness and non-degeneracy. Three approaches to deriving a dimension bound were investigated and found insufficient: a counting argument from the perturbation space (a hypothesis, not a theorem), the link complex of the causal set (triangle-free, giving zero plaquettes), and the order complex (plaquettes-per-edge diverges with density). The topological direction (discrete bundle classification via homotopy groups) remains open but requires nerve-complex constructions not yet implemented.

The representation content of the SM generation and the ``all conditions independent'' selection principle are the two non-derived inputs for the full matter content determination. The number of generations is bounded ($N_g \geq 3$) but not determined. The mass spectrum and Higgs mechanism are not addressed.

\subsection{Open questions}
\label{sec:open}

In order of priority:

\emph{Most pressing.} Can the representation content of one SM generation be derived from a defect-minimality principle on the causal set, or does it remain an external input?  The enumeration (Table~\ref{tab:enumeration}) constrains the options but does not select uniquely without the ``independently constraining'' selection principle.

\emph{High priority.} Can the second-order condition in the Lovelock classification be derived from stability (Ostrogradsky) or causality, rather than imposed?  Can the integration-by-parts step in the Yang--Mills variational derivation be formalized, closing the last gap between the algebraic and manifold-level formulations?

\emph{Medium priority.} Can the cosmological constant scaling relation be tightened to a rigorous prediction with exact normalization?  Can the number of generations be determined --- specifically, does the spatial metric perturbation's eigenvalue structure give $N_g = d$ (a testable conjecture: check whether linearized defect dynamics commutes with $\delta g_{ij}$)?

\emph{Long-term.} Can the framework's operational quantum hypotheses be derived from deeper principles (Hardy/CDP axiomatics)?  Can the semisimple gauge factor be selected by discrete bundle topology (nerve-complex constructions, not yet implemented)?  What determines the mass spectrum and coupling constants?

\section{Conclusion}

We have presented a formally verified parent-structure framework in which the gravity endpoint, gauge structure (including a predicted two-origin reductive form), chirality, matter algebra, a quantum-amplitude layer with a derived propagation rule, anomaly-constrained hypercharges, a CP violation bound, and a strong CP resolution are selected or conditionally induced from a small parent architecture. The classification results show that within the stated classes, the Einstein$+\Lambda$ field equation, the nonabelian Yang--Mills equation, the charge algebra, the Born-type observable, and the propagation rule are each uniquely determined. Each of five framework primitives is proven necessary. A discrete causal-counting model suggests a scaling relation for the cosmological constant at the correct order of magnitude.

The framework identifies a specific path toward further unification: the semisimple factor of the gauge group, the number of generations, and the mass spectrum remain open, with the gauge group question reducible to discrete bundle topology and the generation count connected to the spatial dimension via a testable conjecture. The formal verification ensures that whatever is claimed as derived is derived, and whatever remains assumed is explicitly labeled.
