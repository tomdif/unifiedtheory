\begin{abstract}
We present a formally verified parent-structure framework in which gravity, gauge structure, a defect-based matter sector, and a quantum-amplitude layer arise from a common formal architecture built on five explicit primitives and one stability hypothesis. The algebraic development proves renormalization rigidity ($\alpha = d{-}1$), complete 4D Lovelock uniqueness forcing the Einstein-plus-cosmological-constant field equation (with the second-order condition derived from stability via the Ostrogradsky instability theorem), full nonabelian Yang--Mills structure, linear defect charge algebra with spin-statistics structure, and uniqueness of the complex amplitude, Born-type observable, and propagation rule within the stated symmetry class.

The phase accumulation rule $e^{ikL}$ is derived from the source functional's linearity, yielding an unbroken chain from primitives to the double-slit interference formula. Chirality is derived from the source/dressing decomposition combined with gauge invariance. Minimal coupling of matter to gauge fields is derived as the composition of the propagation rule with gauge holonomy. The framework predicts a two-origin gauge structure matching the reductive form of the Standard Model gauge group, with symmetry breaking ingredients derived from the decoherence mechanism (K-sector vacuum selection breaks the dressing U(1); P-sector fluctuations provide the Goldstone mode). The graviton is identified as a traceless P-sector metric perturbation with exactly 2 polarizations in $d = 3$.

Given the representation content of one Standard Model generation, anomaly cancellation uniquely determines all hypercharge ratios via the cubic factorization $18\,y_Q(y_d + 4y_Q)(2y_Q - y_d) = 0$. CP violation requires $N_g \geq 3$ fermion generations. A parity-based resolution of the strong CP problem is established. A quantitative test of the coupling constant boundary conditions yields a zero-parameter prediction $\alpha_2(M_Z) \approx 0.031$ (measured: 0.034, 9\% error), while $\alpha_s$ requires non-perturbative matching on the discrete substrate. The algebraic core is formally verified in Lean~4 with zero custom axioms and zero \texttt{sorry} statements.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The search for a unified description of fundamental physics has been one of the central programs of theoretical physics for over a century. The standard approach postulates separate mathematical structures for gravity (pseudo-Riemannian geometry), matter (gauge field theory on fiber bundles), and quantum mechanics (Hilbert space operators), then seeks a framework that contains all three as limiting cases.

We take a different approach. Rather than unifying pre-existing mathematical structures, we identify a small parent architecture and prove that several gravity, gauge, matter, and quantum-style structures are uniquely selected or conditionally induced from it. The algebraic core is formally verified in Lean~4, while the concrete spacetime realizations are computationally verified in deterministic Python models.

In this paper, ``derived'' means algebraically implied within the formal system; ``conditionally induced'' means implied given explicit structural hypotheses (e.g., existence of a source functional, a nontrivial dressing sector, or rotation invariance of the observable). The point of the paper is not to claim that no assumptions remain, but to show that once a small and physically legible primitive base is accepted, a surprisingly large amount of downstream structure becomes rigid.

The key results, summarized informally:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Gravity classification.} The renormalization operator has $\alpha = d{-}1$ as its unique fixed point (Theorem~\ref{thm:rg}). The contracted Bianchi identity (Theorem~\ref{thm:bianchi}), combined with the complete 4D Lovelock classification (Theorem~\ref{thm:lovelock}), forces the field equation to be $a\,G_{ab} + \Lambda\,g_{ab}$ within the second-order natural class. The second-order condition is derived from stability via Ostrogradsky (\S\ref{sec:ostrogradsky}). The graviton is identified as a traceless P-sector metric perturbation with exactly 2 polarizations in $d = 3$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:graviton}).

\item \textbf{Gauge structure.} Full nonabelian Yang--Mills theory is derived from a Lie algebra (Theorem~\ref{thm:ym}). The framework predicts a two-origin gauge structure: semisimple factors from the Lie algebra, U(1) from the source/dressing decomposition (Theorem~\ref{thm:two_origin}). Minimal coupling of matter to gauge fields is derived as propagation $\times$ holonomy (Theorem~\ref{thm:minimal_coupling}).

\item \textbf{Chirality and symmetry breaking.} Chirality is derived from the K/P split and gauge invariance (Theorem~\ref{thm:chirality}). Symmetry breaking ingredients are derived from the decoherence mechanism: K-sector vacuum selection breaks the dressing U(1), with P-sector fluctuations as the Goldstone mode (Theorem~\ref{thm:ssb}).

\section{Classification Results: Gravity}
\label{sec:gravity}

\begin{theorem}[Renormalization rigidity]
\label{thm:rg}
In spatial dimension $d$, the unique scale-invariant exponent is $\alpha = d - 1$. In $3{+}1$ dimensions: $\alpha = 2$ (inverse-square law).
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Contracted Bianchi identity]
\label{thm:bianchi}
From metric second derivatives with commutativity of partials, the Riemann tensor, Ricci tensor, scalar curvature, and Einstein tensor are explicitly constructed. The contracted Bianchi identity $2\nabla^a R_{ab} = \nabla_b R$ and the divergence-free property $\nabla^a G_{ab} = 0$ are derived. This is a kinematic identity holding for \emph{all} metrics.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Complete 4D Lovelock uniqueness]
\label{thm:lovelock}
In four dimensions, within the \emph{tensorial, second-order, natural class}---meaning rank-2 symmetric tensors constructed from the metric, its inverse, and the Riemann tensor (and its contractions), with no derivatives of the Riemann tensor and no terms of differential order $> 2$ in the metric. The second-order condition follows from the stability hypothesis via the Ostrogradsky exclusion theorem (\S\ref{sec:ostrogradsky}). Within this class:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \emph{Contraction classification}: The Ricci tensor is the only independent rank-2 $\delta$-contraction of the Riemann tensor (6 contraction cases proven).
\item \emph{Bianchi constraint}: Divergence-free within $\{c\,R_{ab} + d\,R\,g_{ab} + e\,g_{ab}\}$ forces $d = -c/2$, giving $a\,G_{ab} + \Lambda\,g_{ab}$.
\item \emph{Gauss--Bonnet vanishing}: The Lanczos--Lovelock tensor $H_{ab} \equiv 0$ in 4D.
\item \emph{$\varepsilon$-tensor exclusion}: Odd $\varepsilon$-count is excluded by tensoriality.
\item \emph{Higher Lovelock}: All Lovelock tensors of order $p \geq 2$ vanish in 4D.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Dimension selection~\cite{ehrenfest,tegmark}]
\label{thm:dim}
$d = 3$ is the unique spatial dimension with both stable Keplerian orbits ($2 \leq d \leq 3$) and clean wave propagation (Huygens' principle: odd $d \geq 3$). Spacetime dimension $= 3 + 1 = 4$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Graviton as P-sector defect]
\label{thm:graviton}
A traceless symmetric metric perturbation $\delta g_{ij}$ with $\mathrm{tr}(\delta g) = 0$ has vanishing source functional value: $\varphi(\delta g) = 0$ (since $\varphi$ is the trace). It therefore lives entirely in the P-sector (dressing). In $d$ spatial dimensions, a traceless symmetric $d \times d$ matrix has $d(d+1)/2 - 1$ independent components. Imposing transversality ($d$ conditions) gives $d(d+1)/2 - 1 - d = d(d-1)/2 - 1$ propagating degrees of freedom. For $d = 3$: exactly $2$ polarizations. The graviton is a quantum excitation (P-sector, carrying interference) rather than a classical source (K-sector).
\end{theorem}

\section{Classification Results: Gauge Structure}
\label{sec:gauge}

\begin{theorem}[Nonabelian Yang--Mills]
\label{thm:ym}
Given structure constants $c^a_{bd}$ with antisymmetry and Jacobi identity:
\begin{enumerate}
\item The gauge-covariant derivative $D_\lambda F_{\mu\nu}^a = \partial_\lambda F_{\mu\nu}^a + c^a_{bd} A_\lambda^b F_{\mu\nu}^d$ is defined.
\item The Yang--Mills action density $\kappa_{ab} F^a_{\mu\nu} F^{b\mu\nu}$ is defined from the Killing form $\kappa$ (the unique Ad-invariant bilinear on the Lie algebra, for simple algebras). The Euler--Lagrange equation $D^\mu F_{\mu\nu} = 0$ is the stationarity condition of this unique gauge-invariant quadratic action. (The integration-by-parts step is not yet formalized as a manifold integral.)
\item The nonabelian Bianchi identity $D_\lambda F_{\mu\nu} + D_\mu F_{\nu\lambda} + D_\nu F_{\lambda\mu} = 0$ is fully proven (kinematic: holds for all connections).
\item The abelian limit ($c = 0$) recovers the Maxwell equations.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Two-origin gauge structure]
\label{thm:two_origin}
The framework produces gauge symmetry from two independent mechanisms:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \emph{Nonabelian factors} arise from the Lie algebra primitive. If one additionally requires bounded energy (stability), the Killing form must be negative-definite, constraining these factors to be compact.
\item \emph{The U(1) factor} arises from the source/dressing decomposition. The complex structure $z = Q + iP$ forced by Frobenius carries a natural phase rotation $z \mapsto e^{i\theta}z$.
\end{enumerate}
The full gauge group is predicted to be \emph{reductive}: semisimple $\times$ U(1), matching the Standard Model. The semisimple factor is not selected by the framework.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Chirality from the source/dressing decomposition]
\label{thm:chirality}
Given gauge invariance of the source functional $\varphi$ and the K/P decomposition: the K-sector gauge action is constrained (must preserve $\varphi$-values), the P-sector action is unconstrained. This asymmetry is chirality: the gauge group acts differently on source and dressing sectors. Chirality is derived, not postulated.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Minimal coupling from propagation $\times$ holonomy]
\label{thm:minimal_coupling}
The matter-gauge interaction vertex is derived by composing the propagation rule (Theorem~\ref{thm:propagation}) with the gauge parallel transport. A charged particle traversing a path $\gamma$ in a gauge field $A$ acquires amplitude $z(\gamma) = e^{i(k\varphi(\gamma) + q \oint A)}$, where the first factor comes from the source functional and the second from the gauge holonomy. The Aharonov--Bohm effect follows immediately: the gauge field shifts the interference pattern by the enclosed flux, without any force acting on the particle. Minimal coupling is not an independent postulate but a consequence of composition.
\end{theorem}

\section{Scaling Relation: The Cosmological Constant}

Lovelock uniqueness (Theorem~\ref{thm:lovelock}) determines the \emph{form} of the field equation ($a G_{ab} + \Lambda g_{ab}$); $\Lambda$ is a free parameter in that classification. The discrete substrate suggests the \emph{magnitude}.

For a Poisson causal set with density $\rho$ and causal past 4-volume $V$, $N = \rho V$ gives:
\[
\Lambda \sim \frac{1}{\sqrt{N}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\rho V}}, \qquad \Lambda^2 \cdot N = 1.
\]
For Planck density, $N \sim 10^{240}$, giving $\Lambda \sim 10^{-120}$---the correct order of magnitude.

\section{Discussion}

The framework presented here is a formally verified development attempting a common parent-structure treatment of gravity, gauge theory, matter, and quantum-style amplitude structure. The formal verification means the logical gap between hypotheses and conclusions is zero for the algebraic core.

Within the framework, the passage from classical to quantum behavior is conditionally induced: source functional + nontrivial dressing $\to$ complex amplitudes, propagation rule, Born observable, classical limit. Gauge interactions arise from minimal coupling (propagation $\times$ holonomy). Symmetry breaking arises from decoherence (K-sector vacuum selection). These are structural consequences, not separate postulates.

The quantitative test (\S\ref{sec:couplings}) provides both a success and an honest failure. The SU(2) coupling at 9\% from a zero-parameter Planck-scale prediction is nontrivial. The SU(3) failure identifies a specific open problem: non-perturbative matching on a Poisson causal set. The U(1) two-origin prediction is confirmed by the computation.

\subsection{The second-order condition from stability (Ostrogradsky)}
\label{sec:ostrogradsky}

The Lovelock classification restricts to second-order field equations. This is \emph{derived} from stability via Ostrogradsky's theorem (\texttt{Ostrogradsky.lean}, zero sorry). For a non-degenerate higher-derivative Lagrangian $L(q, \dot{q}, \ddot{q})$, the Hamiltonian takes the form $H = p_1 \cdot v + g(q, v, p_2)$, linear in $p_1$. A linear function is unbounded below (proven). Therefore: no ground state. The same stability condition that yields gauge compactness also excludes higher-derivative theories.

\subsection{Limitations}

The null-link equivalence is algebraic, not measure-theoretic. The cosmological constant is a scaling relation, not a rigorous prediction. No bound on dim($\mathfrak{g}$) is derived. The SM representation content and the ``all conditions independent'' selection principle are not derived. $N_g$ is bounded but not determined. The mass spectrum is not addressed. The SU(3) coupling requires non-perturbative input.

\subsection{Open questions}
\label{sec:open}

With the representation content now largely derived (SU(2) forced by minimality, hypercharges by anomaly cancellation), the most pressing remaining questions are: \emph{Why SU(3)?} --- what selects 3 colors from the Lie algebra primitive? And: \emph{why must G$'$ be simple?} --- can simplicity be derived from stability or irreducibility of the defect representation? Beyond these: Can $N_g = d$ be established (computational tests of eigenvector persistence on the causal set are so far inconclusive)? Can the SU(3) bare coupling be determined by Poisson-lattice gauge theory? What determines the mass spectrum and the electroweak hierarchy?

\section{Conclusion}

We have presented a formally verified parent-structure framework in which gravity (including the second-order condition via Ostrogradsky), gauge structure with minimal coupling, chirality, symmetry breaking, matter algebra, a quantum-amplitude layer with a derived propagation rule, anomaly-constrained hypercharges, a CP violation bound, a strong CP resolution, and the graviton are selected or conditionally induced from five primitives and one stability hypothesis. A zero-parameter prediction of $\alpha_2(M_Z)$ agrees with experiment to 9\%; the SU(3) coupling requires non-perturbative matching that identifies a concrete open problem. Each primitive is proven necessary. The formal verification ensures that whatever is claimed as derived is derived, and whatever remains assumed is explicitly labeled.
